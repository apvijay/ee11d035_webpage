Procedure for MS-PhD conversion

Home

  1. Approach your guide (and the guide-you-want-to-be-yours) and talk to him(them) about your interest.
  2. Once he accepts, write a letter to the head of the department stating your interest.
  3. The department office will schedule a conversion meeting with your GTC members.
  4. Prepare a presentation on what your interests are and include the literature you have surveyed (if at all). Better include your list of grades in the first slide(I assume that it is a show-off material).
  5. Once they are happy about your conversion, they will submit a letter of their acceptance to the department.
  6. The department office will carry forward the process to the Academic section.
  7. Academic section takes some time (1.5 months) usually to process the conversion and to get approval from the Senate. Do not go too early nor too late. A researcher should know the exact time to make an appearance.
  8. Once the process is finished, Academic section will inform the department office. Collect the letter from the department office. Guide(s) will be sent a copy too.
  9. Form a DC committee with your guide through the workflow website. Submit the form at the department office.
  10. Go to the library and get the new id card. I thought that this might take a long time. But I ended up with a good chap at the Smart Card room at the library. He asked the details, edited the current smart card information, printed it, transferred my checked-out books to the new account, and hoo, that's it. I got the green card with elephants.
  11. Go to the ccw and update your roll no. in the database. Get new mess card too.
  12. Now scratch your head or scratch your desk for your PhD #youprefer
